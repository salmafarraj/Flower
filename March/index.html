March

Daffodils:
Daffodils, March’s primary birth flower, symbolize new beginnings, hope, and rebirth. Their cheerful yellow blooms are seen as a sign of resilience and joy, brightening the landscape after the long winter months. As a symbol of optimism, daffodils encourage looking forward to fresh starts and future possibilities.

Jonquils:
Jonquils, a specific type of daffodil, also represent March and are closely tied to affection, desire, and inner reflection. Their sweet fragrance and delicate clusters of blooms evoke warmth, sincerity, and deep emotional connections. In the language of flowers, jonquils inspire a sense of renewal that comes not just from nature, but from within the heart.
